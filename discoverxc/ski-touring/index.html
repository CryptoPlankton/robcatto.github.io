Ski touring is done on ungroomed terrain and often goes outside the ski resorts boundaries. You can head out for the morning or the day, or even extend over many days staying in tents or huts along the way. Skis suitable for touring are generally slightly wider than track skis and some have a metal edge for icy conditions. Boots are usually heavier and provide more support and control.

How do I try ski touring?

A good way to explore and learn the skills of ski touring is to join a club that specialises in it or do a local or overseas organised tour. Heading out into the alpine environment requires planning and knowledge of the risks involved.
