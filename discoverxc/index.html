Discover XC

XC was created by Vikings 7000 years ago

That’s right, Vikings in early Scandinavia used skis to get to places since everything was covered in snow. Since then it has branched off into track skiing, ski jumping, telemarking, ski touring and back country skiing. It first came to Australia during the Gold Rush in 1854 and has been gaining popularity ever since.

There's Classic skiing and there's Skate skiing

Cross Country skiing involves two different styles. In Classic style the skis stay parallel and a grip section on the bottom of the ski stops it from gliding backwards. Skating, its newer 80’s sibling uses slightly different skis designed only for gliding and are used in a distinctive V shape similar to in-line skating or ice skating. As you progress and learn more techniques you become more efficient, use less energy and ski faster with minimum effort.

What type of XC Skiing would suit me?

Classic is usually the best starting point for resort skiing. Both classic & skate styles are relatively easy to learn and most people pick up the basics after a couple of lessons. As you progress and learn more techniques you become more efficient, use less energy and ski faster with minimum effort.

Biathlon, a combination of target shooting and racing

Biathlon could be right up your alley. Biathlon is a winter sport that is fun, challenging and exciting. It combines cross-country freestyle skiing and small-bore target shooting. A great way to expand what you can do on xc skis.
