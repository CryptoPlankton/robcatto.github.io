Freestyle technique and target shooting

Biathlon is the only sport that combines the two very diverse disciplines of cross-country skiing and small-bore target shooting. It requires participants to have both stamina and precision to compete in a cross country race and stop to shoot at a series of targets along the way. While most biathlon participants skate ski, you may also use the classic technique.

Most viewed snowsport across the whole of Europe

Biathlon has its origins in the Norwegian Army back in the 18th Century, first making its debut as a sport in the 1960 Winter Games. It's very popular in Europe with competitions attracting thousands of spectators and in Germany it can even beat soccer in the TV ratings!

Mt Hotham Biathlon Range

Biathlon’s winter base in Australia is at the Whiskey Flat Range near Mt Hotham and is the only Biathlon range in Australia. All races are held exclusively at the Whiskey Flat Range near Mt Hotham. Biathlon events are held during the Australian winter snow season and also continue during the off season with various dryland events held around Australia.

Biathlon in Australia

Biathlon has a relatively small but enthusiastic following in Australia and is growing in numbers every year. The Australian Biathlon Association is a not-for-profit organisation, with the objective of fostering the sport of biathlon within the wider community for the enjoyment, challenge and achievement of participants.

Where can racing take you

In Australia there are three main categories. Club races, which are open to all to participants from under 5 to over 80. Championship races, where you can win a state or national title and qualify for National Team level competition and Schools events, which are open to any primary or secondary school students.
