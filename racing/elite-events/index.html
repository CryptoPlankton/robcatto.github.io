Pathways to get to the Olympics

Racing can lead you down number of paths from the Interschools to the Olympics. (link) There are various stepping stones along the pathway including World Junior Championships, World University Games and World Cup. This is an example of one but there are other ways.

World University Winter Games (World Uni Games)

The World University Winter Games (WUWG) are held every two years. Athletes must be enrolled at university and be under the age of 27 to be eligible for selection. The WUWG provide just about the only opportunity outside of the Winter Olympics to compete at an elite international event together with other winter sport disciplines. The selection standard is challenging but achievable, similar to qualifying for the World Junior or U23 Championships.

World Junior / U23 Championships Games

The FIS World Junior and U23 Championships (WJC and WU23C) are held every year. Athletes are eligible to compete in the WJC in the year that they turn 20 and in the WU23C in the year that they turn 23.

Winter Youth Olympic Games

The Winter Youth Olympic Games (WYOG) were held for the first time in Innsbruck in 2012. The number of starters in limited and most nations can enter a maximum of one male and one female athlete. The next WYOG will be held in Lillehammer in 2016.

World Cup

The World Cup circuit takes place every year between November and March, featuring about 22-24 events each season throughout Europe and occasionally in North America.

World Championship

The FIS Nordic World Championships (WSC) are held every two years. The event styles at the WSC and the OWG alternate from year to year, which means that the WSC event styles in years either side of the OWG are the same.

Paralympics

Cross country skiing first appeared at the 1976 Winter Paralympic Games in Örnsköldsvik, Sweden. It is held directly after the Winter Olympic Games at the same venue. The competition is open to athletes with a physical impairment and blindness/visual impairment. Male and female athletes compete in short distance, middle distance and long distance (ranging from 2.5km to 20km) or participate in a team relay using classical or free techniques. Biathlon is also an event at the Paralympics.

Winter Olympic Games

The Olympic Winter Games (OWG) are held every four years. The next OWG will be in Pyeongchang in Korea in 2018. There are 12 cross country skiing medal events at the OWG, six for each gender.
